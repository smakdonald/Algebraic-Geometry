Definition 1. The dimension of a noetherian topological space \(X\text{,}\) written \(\operatorname{dim}(X) \in \{0, 1, 2, \dots, \infty\}\text{,}\) is the supremum of the set of all integers \(n \geq 0\) such that there exists a chain
\begin{equation*} \emptyset \subsetneq Y_0 \subsetneq Y_1 \subsetneq \cdots \subsetneq Y_n \subseteq X \end{equation*}
with each \(Y_i\) an irreducible closed subset of \(X\text{.}\) (The dimension of the empty set is \(-\infty\text{.}\) It is possible that the dimension of a noetherian space is infinite.)
