Definition 1. The Zariski topology on \(\mathbb{A}^n_k\) is the topology in which a subset is closed if and only if it is an affine variety (i.e., cut out by a collection of polynomial equations). Given a variety \(X\) in \(\mathbb{A}^n_k\text{,}\) the Zariski topology on \(X\) is the subspace topology â€” since \(X\) is closed in \(\mathbb{A}^n_k\text{,}\) this means that a subset \(C\) of \(X\) is closed if and only if \(C\) is an affine subvariety of \(X\) (i.e., an affine variety in \(\mathbb{A}^n_k\) that is contained in \(X\)).
